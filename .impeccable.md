## Design Context

### Users
Health-conscious Mac users actively trying to change their diet — dealing with restrictions, dietary approaches, or just boredom with their current meals. They arrive motivated but frustrated by the existing landscape (cluttered recipe sites, subscription apps). They want a tool that respects their intelligence and their privacy.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, honest, opinionated.** Feels like a friend who cares about food and has strong views about how tech should work. Confident without being aggressive. Conversational without being casual. Takes a clear stance ("Old-school macOS app. No login. No cloud.") and doesn't hedge.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Editorial warmth — like a polished indie Mac app with magazine-quality typography
- **References**: Bear, Things, Craft — indie Mac apps with editorial serif typography, generous whitespace, and quiet confidence
- **Anti-references**:
  - Recipe websites (cluttered, ad-heavy, pop-ups — the exact problem the app solves)
  - Generic SaaS landing pages (hero metrics, gradient CTAs, stock photos, "trusted by 10,000+ teams")
  - Health/wellness apps (soft pastels, leaf icons, "your journey" language, generic wellness aesthetic)
- **Theme**: Light mode primary (#FAF8F5 warm off-white), dark mode via prefers-color-scheme
- **Palette**: Warm terracotta accent (#B94D32), warm grays throughout — zero cool tones
- **Typography**: Instrument Serif (headings) + Inter (body) — editorial serif paired with neutral sans

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over polish** — The design should feel inviting and human, not sterile or corporate. Warm colors, editorial typography, generous whitespace.
2. **Confidence over persuasion** — State what the product does clearly and let it speak for itself. No urgency tactics, no social proof gimmicks, no "trusted by" counters.
3. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element should be immediately understandable. No jargon, no ambiguity, no hidden interactions.
4. **Restraint over spectacle** — One accent color, two fonts, minimal animation. Add nothing that doesn't serve the user's understanding.
5. **Respect the user** — No dark patterns, no tracking beyond anonymous analytics, no manipulative copy. The privacy stance isn't a feature — it's the foundation.
